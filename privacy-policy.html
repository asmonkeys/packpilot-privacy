Privacy Policy for PackPilot

Last Updated: February 14, 2026

1. Introduction

PackPilot ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application PackPilot (the "App"). PackPilot is developed and operated by DMCL CPA.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using PackPilot, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use the App.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

PackPilot stores all of your data locally on your device. We collect and store the following information that you voluntarily provide:

  • Trip Information: Trip names, destinations, dates, and travel details
  • Packing Lists: Items you add to your packing lists, categories, and completion status
  • Custom Categories: Any custom packing categories you create
  • Settings and Preferences: Your app preferences and settings

Important: All of this information is stored locally on your device using Apple's Core Data framework. We do not transmit this information to our servers or any third parties.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use PackPilot, certain information may be collected automatically by third-party services:

Advertising Data (Google AdMob)

If you have not purchased the "Remove Ads" feature, we display advertisements through Google AdMob, which may collect:

  • Device Information: Device model, operating system version, unique device identifiers (IDFA/Advertising ID)
  • Usage Data: App interactions, ad impressions, ad clicks
  • Location Data: Approximate location (based on IP address) for ad targeting
  • Advertising Data: Information about ads shown and your interactions with them

This data is collected and processed by Google in accordance with their privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Purchase Information (Apple In-App Purchase)

When you purchase the "Remove Ads" feature:

  • Apple processes your payment information (we never see your payment details)
  • We receive a receipt confirming your purchase from Apple
  • Your purchase is linked to your Apple ID and syncs across your devices

Apple's handling of payment information is governed by their privacy policy: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/

2.3 Analytics and Crash Data

We may collect anonymized analytics and crash data through Apple's built-in analytics framework to improve app performance and stability. This data is aggregated and does not identify you personally.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect in the following ways:

  • To Provide the App's Core Functionality: Your trip and packing list data is used to deliver the core features of PackPilot
  • To Display Advertisements: Device and usage data is used by Google AdMob to show relevant ads (unless you've purchased ad removal)
  • To Process In-App Purchases: Purchase receipts are validated to enable the ad-free experience
  • To Improve the App: Analytics data helps us understand how the app is used and identify areas for improvement
  • To Fix Bugs: Crash data helps us identify and resolve technical issues

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Local Storage

All of your personal packing data (trips, lists, items) is stored locally on your device using Apple's encrypted Core Data framework. This data:

  • Never leaves your device
  • Is not backed up to our servers
  • Is protected by your device's security features (passcode, biometric authentication)
  • May be included in your iCloud backup if you have iCloud Backup enabled (this is managed by Apple, not us)

4.2 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • Using Apple's secure data storage APIs
  • Encrypting sensitive data at rest
  • Following Apple's security best practices
  • Regularly updating the app to address security vulnerabilities

However, no method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

5.1 Third-Party Services

We share information with the following third-party service providers:

Google AdMob (Advertising)

  • Purpose: To display advertisements in the free version of the app
  • Data Shared: Device identifiers, usage data, approximate location
  • Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Opt-Out: You can opt out of personalized ads by purchasing the "Remove Ads" feature or limiting ad tracking in your device settings

Apple (In-App Purchases & App Store)

  • Purpose: To process in-app purchases and distribute the app
  • Data Shared: Purchase information, Apple ID
  • Privacy Policy: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/

5.2 We Do Not Sell Your Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes.

5.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., a court or government agency).

6. Your Privacy Rights and Choices

6.1 App Tracking Transparency (iOS 14+)

When you first use PackPilot, we request your permission to track your activity across other companies' apps and websites for advertising purposes. You can:

  • Allow tracking to support the app with personalized ads
  • Deny tracking to receive less relevant ads
  • Change your choice anytime in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking

6.2 Limit Ad Tracking

You can limit ad tracking on your iOS device:

  • Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking
  • Turn off "Allow Apps to Request to Track"

6.3 Remove Ads Completely

Purchase the "Remove Ads" in-app purchase for a one-time fee to:

  • Eliminate all advertisements
  • Stop data collection by Google AdMob
  • Enjoy a faster, cleaner experience

6.4 Delete Your Data

Since all your packing data is stored locally on your device, you can delete it at any time by:

  • Deleting individual trips within the app
  • Uninstalling the app (this removes all local data)

Note: If you've backed up your device to iCloud, you may need to delete the backup or restore from a backup that doesn't include PackPilot data.

6.5 Access and Portability

All your data is stored on your device and is accessible to you within the app at all times. Currently, PackPilot does not offer a data export feature, but all information is visible within the app interface.

7. Children's Privacy

PackPilot is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us, and we will take steps to delete such information.

8. International Data Transfers

PackPilot is operated in Canada. If you are located outside Canada, please be aware that information we collect (through our third-party service providers) may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own, including the United States. These countries may have different data protection laws than your country.

By using PackPilot, you consent to the transfer of your information to Canada and other countries where our service providers operate.

9. Data Retention

  • Local Data: Your packing lists and trips are stored on your device until you delete them or uninstall the app
  • Advertising Data: Retained by Google AdMob according to their policies (typically 90 days for IP addresses, longer for aggregated data)
  • Purchase Data: Apple retains purchase records according to their policies; we only store the purchase status locally on your device

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by:

Your continued use of PackPilot after any changes indicates your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

PackPilot may contain links to third-party websites or services (such as travel booking sites or destination information). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third parties. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing any information to them.

12. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: You can request information about the personal data we collect, use, and share
  • Right to Delete: You can request deletion of your personal data (delete the app to remove local data)
  • Right to Opt-Out: You can opt out of the "sale" of personal data (we don't sell data, but you can remove ads to stop third-party advertising)
  • Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights

To exercise these rights, contact us using the information below.

13. European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data (you can edit this within the app)
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (uninstall the app)
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data (purchase ad removal)
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  • Right to Object: Object to data processing (turn off ad tracking in device settings)
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for data processing at any time

To exercise these rights, contact us using the information below.

14. Canadian Privacy Rights (PIPEDA)

As a Canadian company, we comply with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). Canadian users have the right to:

  • Access their personal information
  • Challenge the accuracy of their data
  • Withdraw consent for data collection (where applicable)
  • File a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada
